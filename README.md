## Understanding JEDEC Compliance

JEDEC, or the **Joint Electron Device Engineering Council**, is the global organization that develops open standards for the microelectronics industry. Compliance with JEDEC ensures that components adhere to standardized criteria for electrical, mechanical, and thermal characteristics.

### Core JEDEC Standards Implemented by Masline
- **JESD22 Series:** Reliability test methods for semiconductors  
- **JESD625:** Requirements for Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) control  
- **JESD31:** General requirements for handling and storage  
- **JESD47:** Stress-test driven qualification procedures  
- **J-STD-020:** Moisture/Reflow sensitivity classification  

By following these standards, Masline guarantees that its distributed components remain consistent, safe, and suitable for advanced design and manufacturing environments.

## Moisture Sensitivity and Thermal Integrity

Moisture sensitivity is one of the most critical factors affecting semiconductor reliability. Masline follows JEDEC standards J-STD-020 and J-STD-033 to classify and manage components according to their Moisture Sensitivity Levels (MSL).

### Key Moisture Control Procedures
- Accurate MSL classification and labeling  
- Baking and resealing for stored SMD packages  
- Reflow simulation testing for quality verification  
- Controlled reconditioning before shipping  

These processes help customers avoid soldering defects, delamination, or popcorning during reflow soldering operations.
